Discover

Which between the European Union's countries are the best top five against Covid-19?

In this part, our goal it's to select only the best 5 countries (for each wave) between all the 27 countries of the European Union.

There are two main reasons:
1) The first reason is to make the analysis clearer because too many data will give issues to read them in the proper way. In this part we did an exception even if it's a bit messy because we can't exclude countries without a valid reason.
2) The second reason is to demonstrate after this part which from this top 5 countries have used the best factors to fight against the pandemic crisis

To obtain which countries were the best, we constructed 3 different graphs based to death numbers in the European Union's countries:

Furthermore, we decided to divide the data in 3 different waves, because the strategy during time could have changed and so the factors could be different in front of each situation.

The density tells a lot in the crisis due to a higher proximity of people. So we considered the countries with less density but higher death per density to be worse.

4) THE POPULATION:
Having more deaths per million means having managed worse the pandemic crisis.

Analysis: Which factors are used by the top 5 countries?


Stringency and containment index

The Oxford Coronavirus Government Response Tracker (OxCGRT) project calculate a Stringency Index, a composite measure of nine of the response metrics. In July 2022 the OxCGRT implemented data changes which incorporate different policies applying to vaccinated and non-vaccinated people.
The nine metrics used to calculate the Stringency Index are:

  • School closures
  • Workplace closures
  • Cancellation of public events
  • Restrictions on public gatherings
  • Closures of public transport
  • Stay-at-home requirements
  • Public information campaigns
  • Restrictions on internal movements
  • International travel controls

It also includes a 'COVID-19 Containment and Health Response' index which is based on the metrics used in the 'Stringency Index' plus testing policy, contact tracing, face coverings and vaccine policy.

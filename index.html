Analysis: Which factors are used by the top 5 countries?


Stringency and containment index

The Oxford Coronavirus Government Response Tracker (OxCGRT) project calculate a Stringency Index, a composite measure of nine of the response metrics. In July 2022 the OxCGRT implemented data changes which incorporate different policies applying to vaccinated and non-vaccinated people.

The nine metrics used to calculate the Stringency Index are:

  • School closures
  • Workplace closures
  • Cancellation of public events
  • Restrictions on public gatherings
  • Closures of public transport
  • Stay-at-home requirements
  • Public information campaigns
  • Restrictions on internal movements
  • International travel controls

Containement index includes a 'COVID-19 Containment and Health Response' index which is based on the metrics used in the 'Stringency Index' plus testing policy, contact tracing, face coverings and vaccine policy.

What are these factors?

"People fully vaccinated per hundred" and "People vaccinated per hundred" are two important metrics used to measure the progress of COVID-19 vaccination campaigns in a population.
These metrics are expressed as percentages and are used to understand the percentage of a country or region's population that has received at least one dose of a COVID-19 vaccine and the percentage that has received both doses or a complete vaccination series.

Final conclusions

Upon analyzing the data, we observed that strategic factors played a role in shaping the pandemic trends in European Union countries.
However, even when countries like Italy implemented similar or even more stringent measures than the best-performing nations, their outcomes were not necessarily superior.
This leads us to believe that strategic factors alone may not suffice to achieve optimal results in combating a pandemic.
Certainly, there are other critical factors that should not be underestimated, such as the average age of the population, the resilience of the healthcare system, and numerous other variables. Exploring these factors in depth could warrant an extensive and comprehensive study.
